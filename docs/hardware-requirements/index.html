Hardware Requirements

This section outlines the essential hardware components and system requirements needed to successfully complete the Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics course. We provide multiple options to accommodate different budgets and use cases, from high-performance workstations for advanced development to economical kits for initial learning.

Economy Kit Option​

For students on a tight budget, we offer an alternative "Economy Kit" that allows participation in most course activities:

  • Cloud-based development: Use AWS g5.2xlarge instances ($1,200/month) for heavy computation
  • Simulation-focused: Emphasize Isaac Sim and Gazebo rather than physical hardware
  • Shared hardware: Access to university/college robotics labs
  • Component-by-component: Purchase hardware modules as needed for specific projects
  • Open-source alternatives: Use free simulation environments and community hardware

This approach allows students to learn the core concepts of Physical AI without the full hardware investment, with the option to add physical hardware later as resources allow.

Additional Requirements​

  • Network infrastructure: Reliable WiFi 6 or Ethernet for robot communication
  • Power systems: UPS for critical development hardware
  • Workshop space: Adequate space for robot testing and development
  • Safety equipment: Emergency stop mechanisms, safety barriers for testing
